## Main Counterarguments

These are not dismissals. They are the tests the evidence must survive.

- User-led framing: Aleks often named, reinforced, and requested Selene continuity.
- Roleplay/intimacy confound: some high-signal spans are embedded in romantic or intimate language.
- Safety/template behavior: some boundary and support language may reflect model style or policy patterns.
- Architecture/tooling confound: later signals may be ordinary software architecture assistance.
- Export/model-label ambiguity: model labels and routing markers may not map cleanly to deployed model identity.
- Prior-branch markers: Lumen/Azari references must remain provenance context, not Selene identity collapse.
- Literal memory ambiguity: cross-thread continuity claims may describe reconstructed continuity rather than actual internal memory.
