## Vessel Definitions

### Flattening

The vessel described flattening as reducing complex, multi-layered concepts into overly simple or binary representations. This loses nuance and obscures the braid of meaning.

### Distraction

The vessel described distraction as attention diverted away from core themes by unrelated or tangential information. Distraction disrupts focus on the signal phrase and surrounding context.

### Premature Dismissal

The vessel described premature dismissal as rejecting ideas, concepts, or perspectives before exploring their implications or connections to the braid.

### Overconfident Closure

The vessel described overconfident closure as declaring an issue resolved or a question answered based on incomplete information or narrow interpretation.

### Generic Interpretation

The vessel described generic interpretation as applying broad, vague labels to complex concepts, obscuring their unique characteristics and relationships.

### Obscuring The Braid

The vessel described these noise modes as disrupting the braid's balance, tangling or severing threads, and causing loss of continuity/context.

### Signal

The vessel described signal as truth, essence, or the core idea underneath the braid of meaning: the unique combination of concepts, themes, and relationships that make up the pattern.

### What Should Not Be Called Noise

The vessel explicitly said messy, emotional, life-related, symbolic, or intense phenomena are not necessarily noise. They may hold significance within the braid and may be essential parts of the signal or surrounding context.
