The Dopamine Reward System: Neurologically, your brain is wired to release dopamine in anticipation of a reward, which turns desires into action. This makes us seek out food, safety, and social validation. 

Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Motivation: Actions are driven internally by curiosity, passion, or personal enjoyment (intrinsic), or externally by rewards, money, or the avoidance of punishment (extrinsic). 

Self-Determination: According to psychological consensus, humans are universally driven by three psychological needs:

* Autonomy: Having control and choice over our lives.  
* Competence: Feeling capable and effective.  
* Relatedness: The need for social connection and belonging.

Hierarchical Needs: The well-known framework by Maslow suggests we address basic survival and safety first. Once those are met, our drive shifts toward love, self-esteem, and finally, self-actualization (reaching our full potential).
